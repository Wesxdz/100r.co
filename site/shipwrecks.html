shipwrecks

Life isn’t something where honest people who study hard are always successful. You have to compromise as well!

Life was a battle!

-Legendary Moonlight Sculptor, Volume 37 Chapter 1 - Dulmore Fortress's Misfortune

I was born into a religious cult, which complicated my process of individuation. Rather than studying mathematics diligently from a young age to get into MIT or Stanford, I was grappling with my identity pedagogy from the very beginning. After reading the Book of Mormon at the age of 12, I prayed to God with a sincere heart, with real intent, having faith hope in Christ. There was no external response: even if there was, coincidence could never be taken as evidence of a higher power.

Everyone encouraged me to try again, to have faith. I felt cognitive dissonance, I felt like it would be insincere to approach God with a confirmation bias.

Less than a week prior my journey to the divine, I had played Minecraft for the first time. The feeling I had, hiding in a dark cave that first night, listening to the clacking of skeletons outside... It made me feel alive.

God was boring, I wanted to play Minecraft.

15, I read Les Misérables. It was my first encounter with the nuances of morality and the arguments of atheism.

The Jehovah hypothesis tires me, Bishop. It is good for nothing but to produce shallow people, whose reasoning is hollow.

- Les Misérables, Philosophy After Drinking

I didn't realize it at the time: but I was a budding rationalist pursing a self-developed ontology in an homogenous environment of missionary fanatics! When I encountered LessWrong, it was like walking into a nostalgic home.

Due to a missed email, I once ceeded a payment of $10,000 to the void... The lack of savings from my uncompensated work led me to run out of runway for my startup when I conceded to my weakness of physical pain: surrendering thousands out-of-pocket for decade neglected dental care.

I lost my girlfriend, I lost my housing, I lost my sanity.

I constantly struggle with my mental illnesses, the root of which I self-diagnosed as
Hyperfocus on psychologically distant world: flow disrupted via low-level construals. Likely caused by Bipolar I.

I anticipate a Lithium treatment for mood stabilization would substantially improve my well-being, but I refuse to consider it due to the potential longevity reduction.

Being trapped in poverty due to working on humane technology creates a sense of being the only conscious being in a field of automata.


I failed to learn Spanish by making a language learning game Hola Mundo
I have been rejected in many Tech Interviews
I failed to solve housing at Open Source Ecology
I start and abandon too many projects
